About The Dataset

Tuberculosis (TB) is an infectious disease caused by bacteria that most often affects the lungs. It spreads through the air when people with TB cough, sneeze or spit

Human immunodeficiency virus (HIV) is an infection that attacks the body’s immune system. HIV targets the body’s white blood cells, weakening the immune system. This makes it easier to get sick with diseases like tuberculosis, infections and some cancers.

TB is preventable and curable. About a quarter of the global population is estimated to have been infected with TB bacteria. There is no cure for HIV.

The above graphs visualize TB/HIV Reports published by the World Health Organization.

Key Findings

> India's TB and HIV Discrepancy: Despite having a high death rate from tuberculosis, India reports significantly fewer cases of HIV testing and treatment compared to global averages, indicating underreporting or limited testing access.

> Economic Disparity in Impact: TB and HIV disproportionately affect low-income populations in third-world countries, highlighting the role of socioeconomic factors in disease spread and healthcare accessibility.

> Testing and Treatment Gaps: Countries in the African region show a high prevalence of HIV but significantly lower HIV testing coverage (values closer to 0), delaying early detection and treatment.

Proposed Solution

> Scaling Up Testing Programs: Increase accessibility to free or low-cost HIV and TB testing, particularly in low-income and high-prevalence regions. Community-led testing campaigns could improve outreach.

> Integrated Care Models: Combine TB and HIV care programs to address co-infection cases more effectively and ensure that both conditions are managed together.

> Awareness Campaigns: Launch global education programs about prevention, testing, and treatment options for TB and HIV to reduce stigma and increase early testing.
